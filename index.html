About Us

The Philippine Art Foundation (PAF) was founded by the late Dr. Leonor ‘Nong’ Feliciano and was incorporated in 1990 as a non-stock, not-for-profit organization dedicated to the development, preservation and promotion of Philippine Art and art forms.

The Foundation strives to fulfill the following objectives - Research, Education and Training, Creativity and Performance – to nurture national and international appreciation for the Filipino cultural heritage through the arts and its history.

PAF is recognized in the St. Louis community and the U.S. as a premier source of information regarding Philippine art and culture. The Foundation’s Dance Troupe has performed and been invited to return to various international and folklore events around St. Louis and adjacent cities, including programs by the International Institute’s Annual Festival of Nations, and the annual Multi-cultural Revue of the St. Louis County Library. PAF also had a big participation in the celebration of the 2004 World’s Fair Centennial.

Become A Member

Be a member and experience the joy of representing our homeland the Philippines!

Through events performances, whether by dance or other art forms, we nurture our appreciation for the Filipino cultural heritage. Our children learn more about their roots and their history and tradition, they develop friendships among members, and discover the excitement of public performances.

To be a member, we commit to participating in all PAF’s volunteer work and community events programs.

As dance performers, we commit to attending practices regularly to ensure outstanding performance in all events performances.
